Understanding Ovarian Stimulation: A Comprehensive Guide




Ovarian stimulation is a technique used to induce ovulation and increase egg production before assisted conception procedures, enhancing the chances of pregnancy. This process involves medications or injections to stimulate the ovaries, which can be followed by artificial insemination or in vitro fertilization (IVF).

Understanding Ovarian Stimulation: Ovarian stimulation involves tailoring therapy to suit individual patient needs, considering factors like age, ovarian morphology, hormone levels, and responses to previous treatments. Genetic tests may be utilized to personalize pharmaceutical regimens, determining the type and suitable doses of gonadotropins based on a patient's genetic profile.

Procedure for Ovarian Stimulation: The process is typically customized for each patient, involving regular monitoring by a gynecologist through ultrasound scans and blood tests to track follicle growth and maturity. Adjustments to drug doses are made based on the progress observed. Hormone hCG is administered when the follicles reach adequate size to induce final maturation and ovulation, followed by egg retrieval and fertilization in the laboratory.

Duration of Ovarian Stimulation: Ovarian stimulation usually commences around the second or third day of menstruation and continues until approximately day 14. It involves daily injections of gonadotropins, which regulate reproduction, such as FSH and HMG.

Medication for Ovarian Stimulation: Various medications and administration methods are available, with subcutaneous injection being the most common. These hormones are typically combined with medications to prevent spontaneous ovulation of mature follicles before retrieval.

Risks Associated: Ovarian stimulation can cause multiple follicles to grow inside the ovaries, leading to increased size and discomfort. Ovarian torsion, a twisting of the ovary, is a potential risk, urging caution during physical activities. Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome, a severe consequence, is avoidable with proper protocols and medications.

Purpose and Procedure: The primary aim of ovarian stimulation is to boost egg production during ovulation, facilitating higher chances of fertilization. It is performed before artificial insemination to aid sperm reach the fallopian tube and before IVF to retrieve multiple eggs for fertilization in the laboratory.

Components of Ovarian Stimulation: The treatment involves injecting follicle stimulating hormone (FSH) and/or luteinizing hormone (LH) or oral medications to prompt the ovaries to release one or more eggs, eventually leading to egg retrieval for IVF or artificial insemination.
